# Expert Sources — AI-Powered SEO Content Production

## Research Methodology

### Phase 1 — Keyword Validation (DataForSEO API)
Validated 15 seed keywords against real search volume data (US market, Google Ads).
Selected 4 high-signal keywords to drive YouTube discovery:
- **AI SEO** (8,100/mo) — highest volume, broadest coverage
- **GEO SEO** (2,400/mo) — strong upward trend (720 → 3,600 in 12 months)
- **AI search optimization** (1,000/mo) — practitioner-level intent
- **LLM SEO** (880/mo) — niche but stable; signals deep practitioners

Excluded: "AI content production" (10/mo), "automated content SEO" (10/mo) — 
not established community terms despite being descriptively accurate.

### Phase 2-4 — YouTube Discovery & Scoring (YouTube Data API v3)
- Searched 4 validated keywords → discovered 38 unique channels
- Scored each channel on: subscriber base, video frequency (90 days), 
  avg views, engagement rate (likes/views)
- Applied relevance scoring via keyword matching on video titles/descriptions
- Auto-excluded: channels with avg_views < 10 relative to subscriber count 
  (fraud signal), zero engagement, or clearly off-topic content

### Phase 5 — LinkedIn/Newsletter Cross-Reference
LinkedIn has no public discovery API. Candidates sourced via:
- Cross-reference of ≥2 independent industry ranking sources 
  (position.digital, primaryposition.com, ewrdigital.com, digitalmarketingsupermarket.com)
- Conference speaker lists: BrightonSEO 2024-2025, MozCon 2025, SEO Week 2025
- Newsletter subscriber counts where publicly available

### Phase 6 — Merged Scoring & Final Selection
Combined YouTube API metrics + LinkedIn/Newsletter tier assessment:
- LinkedIn S-tier = 10/10, A-tier = 7/10 (based on: AI-SEO focus depth, 
  originality, research output, recency)
- YouTube score = weighted: engagement 35% + recency 30% + subscriber base 35%
- Combined = LinkedIn 50% + YouTube 50% (if both present)

---

## Known Limitations

1. **LinkedIn discovery is not automated.** No public API exists for LinkedIn 
   content discovery. LinkedIn candidates were selected via industry source 
   cross-referencing, not direct engagement data. This introduces recency bias 
   toward well-established names.

2. **YouTube text-matching understates depth.** Relevance scoring via title/
   description keyword matching penalizes experts who discuss AI SEO without 
   using exact keyword phrases in titles (e.g., Aleyda Solís, Julia McCoy).

3. **YouTube channel matching had false positives.** Automated channel lookup 
   for Ryan Law matched "Ahrefs Podcast" instead of personal channel. 
   Mordy Oberstein matched "Sure Oak" (unrelated agency). These were manually 
   corrected in final selection.

4. **YouTube-only candidates are structurally underscored.** Brand/practitioner 
   channels without LinkedIn presence (e.g., Ahrefs, Neil Patel) score lower 
   despite high content quality, due to scoring model weighting LinkedIn tier.

5. **S-tier LinkedIn experts with sparse YouTube presence** (Aleyda Solís, 
   Kevin Indig, Crystal Carter) may appear underscored in automated output. 
   Final selection overrides model ranking based on documented evidence signals.
